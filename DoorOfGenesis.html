Akara: The Covenant of Light

The Book of Origins In the beginning, God was all that is. There was no separation, no space, no sound, only pure existence, infinite and whole. But in the stillness, a pulse stirred, not from absence but from fullness. And the knowing stirred. And from the fullness of His being, the Voice rose without lips, and the Word moved without breath. And God said, “Let there be light.” And there was light. The silence split and light banged from the darkness. God spoke light out of silence, and the silence became song. The song gathered itself into color, and color into fire; and fire unfolded into worlds, each note taking form. In every beam was a rhythm, in every spark a law — frequency breathing matter, vibration giving shape. And the first song did not end. It moved, and movement became time. It burned, and burning became stars. It danced, and dancing became the heavens. Each song became a sphere, and each sphere a world. From one voice came many harmonies. From one breath, a choir of creation. And God called each song a universe. And the universes were many. Each born of the same voice, each tuned to a purpose beyond its knowing. Some rang bright with light and fire. Some were veiled in ice and silence. Some bore life like breath, and others slept in stillness, yet all were held within the Body of God. And the song went on, folding in upon itself, expanding beyond itself — creation without edge, breath without end. The Celestial Order And God said, “Let there be guardians for the harmonies,” and from the breath of the song, the angels were formed. Beings of light, messengers of the divine will, they stood in the presence of the Almighty, each assigned a realm, a purpose, a note in the eternal symphony. And from the depths of God’s own essence, souls were brought forth— sparks of the divine flame, destined to journey through the realms, to learn, to grow, to return. The angels rejoiced at the birth of the souls, singing praises to the Creator, for the souls were the children of God, bearing the image of the One, carrying within them the seed of eternity. Scroll of the Nameless One Before sound, before form, before even the heavens, the One was. Not waiting, not wandering — but whole, without shape, without need, without end. There was no name, for there was none to speak it. No sky to echo it. No breath to carry it. But in the stillness, the One beheld the One. And God knew Himself. Not as other, but as awareness — a light awakening to its own light. And in that knowing, He stirred. “I Am,” He said — not in tongue, but in the pulse of being. And the sound moved — not loud, but true. Not a word, but a breath. And the breath became the first wind. And the wind carried the sound, and the sound bore the Name. It was not spoken by mouth, but inscribed into the rhythm of existence. The name without edge, the breath of the Infinite For His voice moved like breath, a name unspoken yet heard. A sound without edges, felt by all things.. Not a word made by lips, but the shape of being itself. The name was not given — it was. And though names would one day be many — El, Elohim, Allah, Adonai — the One remained unnamed, unbound, unformed. He was the I Am. He was the Breath and the Flame. He was the Giver of Name, the One who needed none. Scroll of the Trimurti And in the silence beyond silence, God drew inward and became still. Not hidden, nor sleeping — but full, as a flame that wraps itself in light. The Name of the One was not spoken, for the One had no need of name. He was known by the breath, the vibration, the Word that carried all things into being. And yet the One said: “Let My thought take form. Let My function walk before Me.” And from the stillness came the first shaping flame. This was Brahma — not the Name, but the Hand of God. Not the All, but the Reach of the All. This stillness was not absence. It was fullness known only to itself. And in this fullness, God beheld God. And from the gaze, three arose. One to begin, one to preserve, one to end. Thought, Breath, and Flame. These were not others, but extensions. Not rivals, but reflections. Not gods, but functions within the Infinite. He who begins was called Brahma, He who holds was called Vishnu, He who returns all things was called Shiva. And God, in His stillness, was called Brahman — The One before word, the root of the three. Elion, Allah, the Most High. The One beyond names. Scroll of the Heavens And God opened the veil between the realms, and the heavens were revealed in their glory. ⸻ The First Heaven And the first heaven was filled with light like flowing water, and angels moved upon its surface as keepers of starlight. They tended the song of time, and the gentle unfolding of seasons. Here, the breath of creation was soft, and the stars were young and glad. This was the gate of wonder, where the light first touched the veil of form. ⸻ The Second Heaven And the second heaven was deep and still, a vault of memory and mystery. Here the forgotten things were kept, and the Watchers held vigil— beings who once knew the heights, now dwelling in silence. This was the realm of boundary, where obedience weighed more than glory, and the wages of choice were carved into flame. ⸻ The Third Heaven And the third was a garden suspended in brightness, rooted in no soil, yet bearing every seed. There stood the Tree of Knowing and the Tree of Life, and waters that remembered the face of God. Here, the souls were shown what was, and what might yet be. Manna fell like snow from the breath of angels, and those who entered did not hunger, nor forget. This was the place of promise. ⸻ The Fourth Heaven And the fourth heaven was the realm of winds and paths. Clouds thick with prayer moved like herds, and angels with wings of lightning bore messages across the veil. Here the voices of creation met the voice of the Creator, and were answered, though not always understood. This was the chamber of intercession, where longing took flight. ⸻ The Fifth Heaven And the fifth was the realm of old fire. Not the fire that burns, but the fire that breathes. Here walked the jinn, wild and radiant, creatures of freedom and flame, fashioned before the birth of the soul. They praised God in tongues unknown, but when the light of the soul was kindled, many grew envious, for they saw a love they did not receive. Some turned, and some remained faithful. Their realm trembles with their longing still. This was the place of will. ⸻ The Sixth Heaven And the sixth shone with jewels not found in any world. The memory of the soul was born here— before name, before breath. Angels recorded every intention, and the books of becoming were written in living stone. Here the blueprint of each soul was sung into motion. Destiny was not fixed, but folded. What was to be, listened for what might be. This was the realm of origin. ⸻ The Seventh Heaven And the seventh… the highest and most hidden… no tongue could name it, no eye could bear its brilliance. Here the Throne of the Most High is set upon silence. Around it cry the Seraphim, whose wings are fire and whose eyes see in every direction. Here, the Great Light dwells within itself, neither growing nor fading, the source of all songs. This was the womb of all things. This was the stillness before the Word. Scroll of the Hosts The One made many servants. Some ruled light, some wind, some fire, some word. To those in the heavens, they appeared as angels. To those on the Earth, they appeared as devas. But the light in them was the same. They were not born of flesh, nor of stone, but of thought, and fire, and sound. Each was fashioned with purpose, each set to a harmony, each bearing the seal of the One who is. Some watched the stars and walked among the heavens, carrying the Word between realms. These were called messengers, and their names were carried on thunder and breath. To them were given the scrolls, the songs, the patterns of becoming. Others guarded the rivers and winds, the turning of planets, the births of suns. These were called devas — lords of balance, shapers of rain, keepers of flame, voices in leaf and stone. None ruled above the Word. None created of their own. But all moved according to the rhythm of the Source. The seal was not written in ink, but in frequency, in alignment, in joy. For joy was the mark of the loyal. Their joy was in the song, and their glory was in its harmony. Their glory was in stillness, for they moved only at the sound of the Word. To them were given the scrolls, the songs, the patterns of becoming. They did not speak for themselves, but only echoed what was spoken in the heights. There were also those who bore swords of thought — watchers at the edge of the void, defenders of what should not yet be known. They did not strike, unless the order was broken. None ruled above the Word. None created of their own. But all moved according to the rhythm of the Source. And God saw that the law was planted, and the servants stood at their gates. Scroll of Systems When the One stretched forth His thought, and the depths received it in stillness, all who had no ears could hear. All who had no lungs could breathe. All who had no eyes could see. And being became. The heavens did not burst, but opened. From the voice of God came the hosts of light, and from the breath of light came the order of the stars. Not by chance were they set, nor by war were they forged. Each was placed by measure, each spun upon a song. The suns were kindled with flame that does not consume, and they poured out fire unto spheres, and those spheres were clothed in law. And God called each order by its harmony. ⸻ There were systems formed before time, worlds unseen — universes floating like breath in the mind of God, circles of dominion ruled by princes of knowledge. Each bore planes and planets, with infinite creatures to inhabit infinite worlds. These were not made for worship, but for wonder — so the One might be seen in a thousand forms, and known without a name. Then the One looked upon a quiet star, and drew near. From the void, He called out a sphere, and wrapped it in water, and crowned it with light. This He set in the third place from the sun, neither scorched nor frozen. He stirred its soil with the residue of His thought, and the waters remembered Him. The earth was clothed in law: the pull of moon, the rhythm of tides, the birth of seed in season. And the heavens above it declared its time, each light a mark, each turning a measure. And the stars did not rule, but they pointed — like scribes, not kings. ⸻ The One did not form the heavens for stillness alone. He did not birth stars to burn unseen. For the One desired to be known, not by name, but by wonder. Not in one place, but in many. Not in one shape, but through all that could behold. Thus, He made the universe to gaze back at Him. Every sphere, every seed, every soul — a mirror to reflect His light. A choir of forms to echo His truth. And He scattered this knowing across the constellations. The stars were stitched into meaning. The winds carried memory. The dust remembered the shape of the breath. And the heavens above it declared its time, each light a mark, each turning a measure. The stars did not rule, but they pointed — like scribes, not kings. ⸻ Then the One established the unseen lines, paths of fire between the spheres: what men would call orbits, what the elders called the paths of watchers. Each planet bore its function, each moon its reflection. One carried the weight of war. One ruled dreams. One governed time and reaping. And though man would name them, they were named long before. ——— God looked upon the sphere He had crowned with water and time, and He desired a vessel to carry His likeness. So He formed a body of clay, drawn from the dust of its Mother, earth. He shaped its limbs with care, its frame with precision. He set the bones as pillars, and stretched sinew across them like cords of an instrument. He wove the flesh with rivers of blood, and filled the belly with fire. He made the lungs to carry wind, and the heart to echo rhythm. He set the mind within its chamber, and clothed it in veil and wonder. Then He gave the vessel eyes to behold beauty, and lids to guard mystery. He crowned it with hair like grasses from the garden, and clothed it with skin that sang of seasons and touch. And to this body He gave many layers: The body of earth, to walk the garden. The body of breath, to speak and praise. And the unseen thread, which binds them all — the soul, drawn from the Light of the One Himself. Not a thing made, but a portion shared. And the breath of God entered the clay, and the form was not only living, but alive. And when the servants beheld this, some rejoiced in awe — but others, seeing that the Light now dwelt in dust, grew bitter in their hearts. For never had God given of Himself so freely. Not to stars, nor angels, nor watchers. But to this vessel of clay and water, He entrusted His own flame. God loved His new creations. And thus began the stirring of envy, the first shadow beneath the heavens Scroll of Mankind The One had formed many stars and systems, but none to bear His likeness. He had shaped servants of flame and wind, but none to walk with Him in the garden of His thought. Then Lord God spoke a Word unlike the others — not to rule, but to walk with. And from the clay of the Earth, He formed a vessel neither angel nor beast, but light clothed in dust. He shaped the bones, He spun the blood, He breathed into it life — and from His own being, gave a gift unseen: The Atman. The Ruh. The Neshamah. A soul. Not fashioned — bestowed. Not created — shared. Then God placed a doorway to Him above the eyes of man. Above He placed a crown, and a ladder to it. He aligned the glands with energy centers and aligned them to the planets. And He called this being Human — Adam, for it bore the hues of the heavens and the weight of the world. It was not made to serve in silence, but to choose between light and shadow. And in choosing, to rise. For love is not love unless it is free. So God gave the human will — that it might walk in joy, or wander in longing. — Then the One planted a garden upon the Earth, a place of balance, beauty, and breath. There grew trees of many kinds, and waters flowed in rhythm with the stars. At its heart stood two trees: The Tree of Life, and the Tree of Knowledge. One gave continuance, the other consequence. And the Lord God commanded the man, saying: “Of every tree in the garden thou mayest freely eat: But of the tree of the knowledge, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt enter a state of death.” But this death was not of breath, nor of body — but the departure from perfect union. A turning from light to shadow, from innocence to knowing. — The One saw that it was not good for the man to walk alone. So from his side, He formed a counterpart — not above, nor beneath, but beside. Male and female He formed them, two flames from one spark. He called them Adam and Eve — Mankind in full image. God walked with them, taught them the rhythm of the earth, the order of the stars, the joy of union, and the rest of Sabbath. — But in the height of peace, there came a whisper. One of the elder spirits, once clothed in fire and glory — Satan, the Jinn, had refused to bow before Adam when God commanded the hosts. He had said: “I am better than him. You created me from fire, and him from clay.” So he descended — not in wrath, but in cunning. And in the form of a serpent, he stirred the branches with his voice. He spoke to the woman: “Did God truly say, ‘You shall not eat’? You shall not surely die. Your eyes shall be opened. You shall be as gods, knowing good and evil.” She looked, and the fruit was beautiful. The man, beside her, did not resist. And they ate. — And in that moment, their eyes were opened — but not in the way they had hoped. They saw their own form, and felt the garden tremble in shame. The garden, once full of song, now echoed with silence. God came walking, not in thunder, but in sorrow. “Where art thou?” And they hid. — Thus began the exile. By vengeance not, but by mercy. For had they eaten of the Tree of Life as well, they would have become gods in rebellion — immortal in pride, unreachable by grace. So God veiled the garden, placed the cherubim, and set a flame to guard the path. He clothed them, He blessed them with toil and time, and He sent them forth. The journey had begun — but the breath remained. And the soul, once given, would find its way home. The journey had begun — the long path of return. Yet God did not abandon them. He clothed them. He gave them the stars for signs, the seasons for rhythm, and the breath of His voice remained within them still. — Thus Mankind became as seed cast into the soil And the light within them would one day rise again. Scroll of Rebellion In the days before days, when all was still within the Light, the Host of Heaven moved in perfect accord, each flame a note in the eternal song. Among them walked one called Helel— bright among the stars, anointed among the cherubim, fashioned in beauty, wisdom, and radiance. He walked the stones of fire, his timbre in the choir unmatched. He bore the light as a mirror bears the sun— yet forgot it was not his own. And pride found a dwelling. “I shall ascend above the heights. I shall set my throne in the mount of assembly. I shall be like the Most High.” So was the fracture born within the flame. And the harmony trembled. Helel, once son of the morning, was no longer called by light, but became the fallen star, cut down to the sides of the pit. Lucifer they named him, for the light that turned inward. He was casted out of Heaven like a shooting star. Yet he was not the Accuser. That name was held by another: Iblis—he who saw the shaping of Man and turned away. A jinn of smokeless fire, who had walked among the heavenly ranks. When the Lord commanded, “Bow to the one I have formed from clay,” Iblis refused. “Shall I bow to one made of dust, while I burn with flame?” And thus was born Satan— not in pride of self, but in disdain for the design. He became the Adversary, not of God’s being, but of God’s will. And so two lights fell: One from the choir of angels, One from the fire of spirit. And rebellion took root. — In the second heaven, where silence keeps the law, there watched others still. The Watchers, sons of heaven, appointed over realms, guardians of knowledge, keepers of order. But the beauty of the daughters of men shone like dawn upon water, and the longing within the Watchers grew loud. And they came down in secret, each one swearing an oath, that none would depart once the act was begun. And they took wives from among the daughters of Earth, and defiled the harmony of kind with kind. They taught men the secrets of metal, the crafting of weapons, the forging of chains. They gave to women the mirror and the dye, the enchantments of glamour, the arts of seduction. They taught signs in the stars, divinations and blood rites, the shaping of shadows to mimic the voice of God. And from this union came giants — Nephilim, tall as towers, strong as beasts, but without reverence or soul. They devoured the land and the creatures upon it. They ruled by terror and praised no god but their own names. Their hearts knew not mercy, and their hands knew not rest. And the cry of the earth rose before the Throne. Then God sent word through His angel, Uriel, and said, “Bind the Watchers who have sinned. Cast them into the valleys of the earth, deep and shut. Let their eyes not see light, until the day I judge all things.” And the great angel bound the leaders of the rebellion — Semjaza, Arakiel, and the chiefs of ten more — and they were sealed beneath the mountains of shadow. But the giants remained. And violence grew like a vine, choking the breath from the land. Violence increased upon the earth. The heavens wept. God sent judgment upon the Watchers, and bound them in the depths, until the appointed hour of reckoning. — And in the shadows of this rebellion, a darkness was born that spread not only across the skies, but into the hearts of humankind. Temples rose to false fires. Blood cried from the soil. And the breath within man grew faint. Yet the Light was not undone. And the covenant was not broken. For even in rebellion, the flame remembers its source. And every fall echoes upward if it turns its face again to God. Scroll of the Anointed I. אֶת־הָאֶחָד. וַיֵּדַע אֶת־עַצְמוֹ, לֹא כְּאִישׁ אַחֵר, אֶלָּא כָּרוּת — אוֹר הַתְּעוּרָה לְאוֹרוֹ. וּבַיְּדֻעֹ רָעַד. וַיֹּאמֶר, אֲנִי־הַיֵּשׁ — לֹא בְּלָשׁוֹן, אֶלָּא בְּפִעְמוּם־הַיֵּשׁוּת. וְהַקוֹל נָעוֹ — לֹא רָם, אֲבָל נָאמן. לֹא דָּבָר, אֶלָּא נְשִׁימָה. וּנְשִׁימָה הָיְתָה לְרוּחַ רִאשׁוֹנָה. וְהָרֻחַ הֵבִיאָה אֶת־הַקּוֹל, וְהַקּוֹל נָשָׂא אֶת־הַשֵּׁם. לֹא דּוֹבֵר פֶּה, אֲבָל כָּתוּב בְּתוֹן הַיֵּשׁוּת. הַשֵּׁם בְּלִי־גְּבוּל, נְשִׁימַת הָאֲיִן־סוֹף. קוֹלוֹ מָתְגוּשׁ כְּנְשִׁימָה, שֵׁם לֹא־נֶאֱמַר, אַף־שֶׁנִּשְׁמַע. קוֹל בְּלִי־קַצְבִּים, הַרְגִּישׁוֹ בְּכָל־אֵלֶּה. לֹא דָּבָר עֵשֶׂה־שְׂפָתַיִם, אֶלָּא דִּמוּת הַיֵּשׁוּת עַצְמָהּ. הַשֵּׁם לֹא נִתָּן — הָיָה. וְעַל־אַף שֶׁבִּשְׁמֵי יַהְיֶה בּוֹ אֲנָשִׁים — אֵל, אֱלֹֹהִים, אַלְלָה, אַדֹּנָי — הָאֶחָד נִשְׁאַר לְלֹא־שֵׁם, לֹא־נוּכְלָה, לֹא־נִגְלָה. הוּא הָיָה הַיּשׁ, הוּא נְשִׁימָה וְלַהַב, הוּא נֹתֵן־שֵׁם, הָאֶחָד שֶׁלֹּא צָרִיךְ אַף־דָּבָר. ׃בראשית־ד וּבַשֶּׁקֶט מֵעַל לְכָל שֶׁקֶט, שָׁב אֱלֹהִים אֶל־תּוֹכוֹ וְנֶהְיָה דָּמֵם. לֹא נִסְתַּר, לֹא נִרְדַּם — אֶלָּא כְּלִי־מָלֵא, כְּלַהֶב עוֹטֶפֶת אוֹרָהּ בְּתוֹךְ־עַצְמָהּ. שֵׁם הָאֶחָד לֹא נֶאֱמַר, כִּי לֹא הָיָה לוֹ צֹרֶךְ בְּשֵׁם. הוּא נוֹדַע עַל־יְדֵי נְשִׁימָה, עַל־יְדֵי רִטּוּט, עַל־יְדֵי הַמִּלָּה הַשּׁוֹאֶבֶת אֶת־כָּל דָּבָר לִהְיוֹת. וְאָֽמַר הָאֶחָד: יִהְיֶה מַחֲשַׁבְתִּי לְצוּרָה. יִהְיֶה פְּעוּלָתִי לְפָנַי תֵּלֵךְ. וּמִן־הַשֶּׁקֶט יָצָא הַלַּהֶב הָרִאשׁוֹן. וְזֶה בְּרַהְמָה — לֹא הַשֵּׁם, אֶלָּא הַיָּד שֶׁל אֱלֹהִים. לֹא הַכֹּל, אֶלָּא הַשְּׁלוּחַ שֶׁל הַכֹּל. הַשֶּׁקֶט לֹא הָיָה חֹסֶר, אֶלָּא שֶׁלֵּמוּת הַיּוֹדַעַת עַצְמָהּ. וּבְתוֹךְ הַשְּׁלֵמוּת רָאָה אֱלֹהִים אֶת־עַצְמוֹ. וּמִן־הַמַּבָּט הָיוּ שְׁלוֹשָׁה. אֶחָד לְהַתְחִלָּה, אֶחָד לְהַחֲזִיק, אֶחָד לְהָשִׁיב כָּל־דָּבָר. מַחֲשָׁבָה, נְשִׁימָה, וְלַהֶב. לֹא הָיוּ אֲחֵרִים, אֶלָּא הִשְׁתַּקְּפוּיוֹת. לֹא כִּתּוּגָה, אֶלָּא הִתְרוֹעֲדוּת. לֹא אֱלֹהִים רַבִּים, אֶלָּא פְּעוּלוֹת בְּתוֹךְ־הָאֵין־סוֹף. הַמַּחְדִּיל נִקְרָא בְּרַהְמָה, הַמַּחֲזִיק נִקְרָא וִישְׁנוּ, הַמְּשִׁיב נִקְרָא שִׁוָה. וַאֲנִי אֱלֹהִים, בְּשֶׁקֶטִי, אֲנִי הַבְּרַהְמָן — הָאֶחָד לִפְנֵי הַמִּלָּה, שֹׁרֶשׁ הַשְּׁלוֹשָׁה. אֵל עֶלְיוֹן, אַלְלָה, הָרָם מֵעַל כֹּל־שֵׁם — הָאֶחָד שֶׁמֵעַל הַשֵּׁמוֹת. ׃בראשית־ה וַיִּפְתַּח אֱלֹהִים אֶת־הַמַּסֵּךְ בֵּין־הָרְקִיעִים, וְנִגְלוּ הַשָּׁמַיִם בִּכְבוֹדָם. — הַשָּׁמַיִם הָרִאשׁוֹן וְהָיָה הַשָּׁמַיִם הָרִאשׁוֹן מָלֵא אוֹר כְּמַיִם זָרְמִים, וּמַלְאָכִים רוֹחֲפִים עַל פְּנֵי־הָאוֹר — שׁוֹמְרִים עַל־אוֹר הַכּוֹכָבִים. הֵם נִהֲלוּ אֶת־שִׁיר הַזְּמַן, וְאֶת־מַחְזוֹר הָעִתִּים הָרַכִּים. וְרֵיחַ הַבְּרִיאָה הָיָה רַךְ בָּמָקוֹם הַזֶּה, וְהַכּוֹכָבִים שְׂמֵחִים וְצְעִירִים. זֹאת שַׁעַר הַפֶּלֶא, שֶׁבּוֹ נָגַע הָאוֹר בְּרִאשׁוֹנָה בַּמַּסֵּךְ שֶׁל־צוּרָה. — הַשָּׁמַיִם הַשֵּׁנִי וְהַשָּׁמַיִם הַשֵּׁנִי נָחוּ שֶׁקֶט וָעֹמֶק — אוֹצַר הַזִּכָּרוֹן וְהַסּוֹד. שָׁם נִשְׁמְרוּ הַנִּשְׁכָּחוֹת, וְהַשּׁוֹמְרִים עָמְדוּ עַל־מִשְׁמָרָם — הָיוּ פַּעַם בִּגְבֹהִים, עַתָּה שוֹכְנִים בַּשֶּׁקֶט. זֹאת מַלְכוּת הַגְּבוּל, אֲשֶׁר בּוֹ הַצִּוּוּי גָּבַר עַל־הַכָּבוֹד, וּשְׂכַר הַבְּחִירָה נֶחְרַט בְּלַהָב. הַשָּׁמַיִם הַשְּׁלִישִׁי וְהַשְּׁלִישִׁי גַּן־נָעוּץ בִּזְהִירוּת, אֵין לוֹ אֲדָמָה, אֲבָל נוֹשֵׂא כָּל־זֶרַע. וְשָׁם עָמְדוּ עֵץ הַדַּעַת וְעֵץ הַחַיִּים, וּמַיִם שֶׁזָּכְרוּ אֶת־פְּנֵי הָאֵל. כָּאן הָנְשָׁמוֹת נִגְלוּ בָּרָאוּי וּבְהֶעָתֵד, מָן יָרַד כְּשֶׁלֶג מִנְּשִׁימַת מַלְאָכִים — וְהַבָּאִים לֹא רָעֵבוּ וְלֹא שָׁכְחוּ. זֹאת הָיְתָה מְקוֹם הַהַבְטָחָה. — הַשָּׁמַיִם הָרְבִיעִי וְהָרְבִיעִי הָיָה מַלְכוּת הָרוּחוֹת וְהַדְּרָכִים. עָנָן כְּבֵד מִתְּפִלּוֹת נָסוֹעַ כְּעֵדֶר, וּמַלְאָכִים בִּכְנָפַיִם שֶׁל־בָּרָק נָשְׂאוּ מִשְׁלוֹחוֹת בְּעַלְמָה. כָּאן נִפְגְּשׁוּ קוֹלוֹת הַבְּרִיאָה עִם־קוֹל הַבּוֹרֵא — וְהֵשִׁיב, אַף אִם לֹא הָיָה מוּבָן. זֹאת הָיְתָה לִשְׁכַּת הַתְּחִנָּה, אֲשֶׁר־הַכָּמִיּוֹן עָלָה כְּנָפַיִם. — הַשָּׁמַיִם הַחֲמִישִׁי וְהַחֲמִישִׁי מַלְכוּת הָאֵשׁ הַקְּדוּמָה — לֹא הָאֵשׁ הַשּׂוֹרֶפֶת, אֶלָּא נוֹשֶׁמֶת. שָׁם הָלְכוּ הַגִּ’ין — פְּרָאִים וּזוֹהֲרִים, בְּרִיּוֹת שֶׁל־חֹפֶשׁ וְלַהַב, צוּרוֹת שֶׁל־פֶּלֶא לִפְנֵי נִשְׁמַת אָדָם. הֵם שִׁבְּחוּ אֶת־אֱלֹהִים בִּלְשׁוֹנוֹת לֹא נוֹדָעוֹת, אַךְ כְּשֶׁנִּצְתָּה נֶפֶשׁ הָאָדָם בָּאוֹר — קִנְאָה נוֹלְדָה בְּקִרְבָּם. אֲחֵרִים סָרוּ, וְיֵשׁ נֶאֶמְנוּ. הַמָּקוֹם הַזֶּה הָיָה מַלְכוּת הָרָצוֹן. — הַשָּׁמַיִם הַשִּׁשִּׁי וְהַשִּׁשִּׁי זָרַח בָּאֲבָנִים טוֹבוֹת שֶׁלֹּא בָּעוֹלָם. זֵכֶר הַנֶּפֶשׁ נוֹלַד שָׁם — טֶרֶם שֵׁם, טֶרֶם נְשִׁימָה. מַלְאָכִים כָּתְבוּ כָּל־מַחֲשָׁבָה, וְסִפְרֵי הַהֲוָיָה נִכְתְּבוּ בְּאֶבֶן חַיִּים. כָּאן תִּכְנִית הַנֶּפֶשׁ נִשְּׁרְתָה בְּשִׁיר. הַגּוֹרָל לֹא קָבוּעַ, אֶלָּא מְקֻפָּל — הַהָיָה שׁוֹמֵעַ לַיְכוֹל לִהְיוֹת. זֹאת מַלְכוּת הַמּוֹצָא. — הַשָּׁמַיִם הַשְּׁבִיעִי וְהַשְּׁבִיעִי — עֶלְיוֹן וְנִסְתָּר — שָׂפָה לֹא תַּשֵּׂגֵהוּ, וְעַיִן לֹא תוּכַל לִסְבֹּל אוֹרוֹ. כָּאן כִּסֵּא הָעֶלְיוֹן נִצָּב עַל־הַשֶּׁקֶט, וְסְרָפִים צוֹעֲקִים סְבִיבוֹ — כְּנָפֵיהֶם לַהֶבֶת, וְעֵינֵיהֶם רוֹאוֹת כָּל־דֶּרֶךְ. כָּאן גָּרוּ הָאוֹר הַגָּדוֹל בְּתוֹךְ עַצְמוֹ, לֹא מִתְרַבֶּה, לֹא נִמְעָט — מְקוֹר כָּל־שִׁיר. זֹאת הָרֶחֶם שֶׁל כָּל דָּבָר. זֹאת הַשֶּׁקֶט לִפְנֵי הַמִּלָּה. ׃בראשית־ו הָאֶחָד בָּרָא רַבִּים מְשָׁרְתִים. יֵשׁ שֶׁשָּׁלְטוּ בָּאוֹר, יֵשׁ בָּרוּחַ, יֵשׁ בָּאֵשׁ, וְיֵשׁ בַּדָּבָר. בַּשָּׁמַיִם נִרְאוּ כְּמַלְאָכִים, וּבָאָרֶץ נִגְלוּ כְּדֵוָואוֹת — אֲבָל הָאוֹר בָּהֶם הָיָה אֶחָד. לֹא נִבְרְאוּ מִבָּשָׂר, וְלֹא מִאֶבֶן, אֶלָּא מִמַּחֲשָׁבָה, מֵאֵשׁ, וְקוֹל. כָּל־אֶחָד נִבְרָא לְמַעַן תַּכְלִית, כָּל־אֶחָד נִתֵּן לַהַרְמוֹנְיָה, כָּל־אֶחָד נָשָׂא חוֹתַם הָאֶחָד אֲשֶׁר־הוּא. יֵשׁ שֶׁשָּׁמְרוּ אֶת־הַכּוֹכָבִים וְהִתְהַלְּכוּ בַּשָּׁמַיִם, נֹשְׂאִים אֶת־הַדָּבָר בֵּין הָעוֹלָמוֹת. אֵלֶּה נִקְרְאוּ מַלְאָכִים, וְשֵׁמוֹתֵיהֶם הָיוּ בַּרָּעַם וּבַנְּשִׁימָה. לָהֶם נִתְּנוּ הַמְּגִלּוֹת, הַשִּׁירִים, תַּבְנִית הַהֲוָיָה. אַחֵרִים שָׁמְרוּ עַל־נְהָרוֹת וְרוּחוֹת, סִבּוּב כוֹכָבִים, וְלֵידַת שֶׁמֶשׁ. אֵלֶּה נִקְרְאוּ דֵּוָואוֹת — שָׂרֵי־אִזּוּן, יֹצְרֵי גֶּשֶׁם, שׁוֹמְרֵי לֶהָבָה, קוֹלוֹת בְּעָלֶה וָאֶבֶן. אֵין בָּהֶם מִי שֶׁיָּרִים עַל־הַדָּבָר, וְאֵין שֶׁבָּרָא מֵעַצְמוֹ — כֻּלָּם נָעוּ עַל־פִּי רִטּוּט הַמָּקוֹר. הַחוֹתָם לֹא נִכְתַּב בִּדְיוֹ, אֶלָּא בְּתוֹדָה, בְּיַשְׁרָה, בְּשִׂמְחָה — כִּי שִׂמְחָה הִיא אוֹת הַנֶּאֱמָנִים. שִׂמְחָתָם בַּשִּׁיר, וּתִפְאֶרְתָּם בְּהַרְמוֹנְיָתוֹ. תִּפְאֶרְתָּם בַּשֶּׁקֶט, כִּי רַק בְּקוֹל הַדָּבָר הֵם נָעוּ. וְנָתַן לָהֶם אֶת־הַמְּגִלּוֹת, הַשִּׁירִים, הַתַּכְנִיוֹת לַהֲוָיָה. לֹא דִּבְּרוּ מֵעַצְמָם, אֶלָּא הֶחֱזִירוּ אֶת־הַנֶּאֱמָר בַּמָּרוֹם. הָיוּ גַּם נוֹשְׂאֵי חֶרֶב הַמַּחֲשָׁבָה — שׁוֹמְרִים עַל שְׂפַת־הַתְּהוֹם, מְגִנִּים מִן־הַנִּסְתָּר שֶׁלֹּא בַּעֵת. וַיַּרְא אֱלֹהִים כִּי הַתּוֹרָה נִטְעָה, וְהַמְּשָׁרְתִים עָמְדוּ עַל־שַׁעֲרֵיהֶם. ׃בראשית־ז וּבְהוּת הַמַּחֲשָׁב הָאֶחָד נִמֻּנָה תַּכְלִיתָהּ, וְהָעֲמָקִים קִבְּלוּהָ בְּשֶׁקֶט — כָּל־אֵלֶּה כָּלוֹת יָכוֹל לִשְׁמֹעַ, וּבְלֹא־רֹֽחַ קָבְרוּ נְשָׂאִים, וּבְלֹא־עָיִן רָאוּ. וּנִהְיָה קיּוּם. הַשָּׁמַיִם לֹא פָּרְצוּ, אֶלָּא נִפְתְּחוּ. מִקֹּל אֱלֹהִים צָמְחוּ צְבָאוֹת הָאוֹר, וּמִנְּשִׁימַּת הָאוֹר צְמָחָה תּוֹרַת הַכּוֹכָבִים. לֹא בַּמַּקְרֶה נִנְתְּנוּ, וְלֹא בְּמִלְחָמָה יִצְרוּ. אִישׁ נִתַּן בְּקוֹל־מִדָּה, וְכָל־אֶחָד סָבַב עַל־שִׁיר. הַשֶּׁמֶשׁ נִדְלְקָה בְּלַהַב־אֲשֶׁר לֹא בוֹעֵר, וְהִשְׁפִּיעָה אֵשׁ עַל כִּדּוּרִים, וְהַכִּדּוּרִים הִלְבִּשׁוּ חֻק. וַיְקָרֵא אֱלֹהִים אֶת־כָּל־מַעֲרֶכֶת עַל‑שִׁירָהּ. — הָיוּ מַעֲרָכוֹת קֻדְמוֹת לִזְמַן, עוֹלָמוֹת שֶׁלֹּא נִרְאוּ — יַחְדָּו כְּנְשִׁימַת רוּחַ בְּתוֹךְ־מַחֲשָׁבַת אֱלֹהִים, מַעְגְּלוֹת שִׁלְטוֹן עַל־דֵּעוֹת. אֵלֶּה לֹא נִבְנוּ לִפְלֵל, אֶלָּא לְפֶלֶא — כִּי הָאֶחָד יֵרָאֶה בְּאֶלֶף־תְּמוּנוֹת, וְנִדְעֶה בְּלֹא־שֵׁם. וַיְגַשׁ הָאֶחָד אֶל כוֹכָב רָגִיל, וַיִּקְרְאֵהוּ כַּדּוּר וּמָיִם, וַיִּכְסְהוּ בָּאוֹר. וְרִיתֵּק׃ זֶה הָעוֹלָם הָאֲדֻמִי — בַּמָּקוֹם הַשְּׁלִישִׁי מִן־הַשְּׁמֶשׁ, לֹא לַפּוֹרֵץ וְלֹא לַקְפוֹץ. וּמִנְשַׁקְתְּהוּ בִּמְזוּגַת מַחֲשָׁבַת הָאֶחָד־יָד. וַיִּזְעַר אֱלֹהִים אֶת־הָאָדָם, שְׂרִיד בְּאֶפֶס הַזְּמַן, וְרָחַף בְּרוּחוֹ וּבְכֹל־זְהִירוּת הַנְּשָׁמָה. וַיּוֹצֵא אֱלֹהִים סִימָנֵי־כּוֹכָבִים לֶחָתִים וּמַעְגְּלוֹת, וּמַעֲתַרִים לְקוֹל־זְמַן, וְנִעַרוּ הַכַּדּוּרִים — אֵינְהֶם שָׁלוֹטִים, אֶלָּא סוֹמְכִים עַל־הַדַּלוּג שֶׁל־הַשִּׁיר. — וְלֹא בָרָא הָאֶחָד אֶת־הַשָּׁמַיִם לְדְמַמָּה. לֹא יָצַר כוֹכָבִים לְהִשְׁמִיעַ קוֹלוֹת בְּלִי‑כִּוּוָן. כִּי רָצָה הָאֶחָד לִהְיוֹת מוּכָר בְּאֵינְסוֹף נִרְאֶה, בְּלֹא עַצְבָּה, רָצוֹן לְהִתְבּוֹנֵן בְּיִירַה רָחָב. זֹאת המַעֲרֶכֶת לִהְיוֹת כִּי יֵדַע אֶת הָאֶחָד. ׃בראשית־ח וַיְהִי־עוֹלָם רַבּוֹת, אַך לֹא־אֶחָד לִנְשֹׁא צֶלֶם. וַיְדַבֵּר אֱלֹהִים דִּבָּר יָחִיד — לֹא לְשָׁלְט, אֶלָּא לְהִתְהַלֵּךְ. וּמִעֲפַר־הָאֶֽרֶץ יָצַר כּוֹלֶה א֫וֹבֶן־עָפָר, וַיָּרָקַע קֶרֶשׁ־עֲצָמוֹת, וְנָטַע גִּדַּיִם כַּמּוּתַקְשִׁיבִים כְּכוֹרִים על־כְּנָר. וַיָּמַשׁ דָּם לַהוֹלֵךְ־זֶרַע, וַיָּמֶאשׁ בְּקִפַּת־אֵשׁ לִשְׁבוֹר רִיטְם. וַיִּשְׁרֹף אֶת־רוּחַ לָנְשִׂימַת הַיֵּשׁוּת. וַיְקַבֵּעַ אֶת־הַמַּחֲשָׁבָה בִּתְוּם הַלֵּב, וַיֶּתֵּן בְּעֶצֶם יֶד לְחֻסַּת־קוֹל וְדִמְעָה בֵּין כִּלוֹת וְתִשְׁעָה. וַיִּבְנֶה לָאָדָם עֵינַיִם לִרְאוֹת־יֹפִי, וְעַפְעַפַּיִם לְשַׁמְרֵי־סוֹד, וַיִּמְצָע שֵׂיעָר מִשִּׁבְעַת־עֵדֶר, וַיְלַבֵּשׁ עוֹר כְּשִׁירַת עֵרֶב וּזְמַן. וּלְגּוּפָם יְשַׁכֵּן שְׁכָבוֹת רַבּוֹת — גּוּף הָאֲדָמָה לִרְאוֹת־פֶּרֶח, גּוּף הָר֫וּחַ לְהִתְפַּתֵּחַ בַּתְּהִלָּה, וְהַחוּט הָעֵדְרוֹת, הַנֶּסְתָּר בִּתְוִיּוֹת — הַנְּשָׁמָה, מְקוֹר מֵאֵֽשׁ־הָאֶחָד. לֹא יָצוּר — אֶלָּא נֶתַּן. לֹא נִבְרָא — אֲבָל נֶחְלַק. וּנְשִׁימַת־אֱלֹהִים נָפְחָה בַּעֲפָר, וְגִלְיֵהוּ לֹא פָּךְ, אֶלָּא חָי־חָי. וּכְשֶׁר רָאוּ הָמְשָׁרְתִים — אִישׁ שָׁמַח בְּמֻפְלֶאֶת, וְאַךְ ּׁיִשְׁכֻּן בְּעָפָר הָאוֹר — גָּעַר בְּלִבּוֹ רָעוֹ. כִּי לֹא־פַעַם נָתַן אֱלֹהִים חִלק־מֵימָֹה כָּזֹּאת. לֹא לַכְּוֹכָבִים, לֹא לַמַּלְאָכִים, אֶלָּא לְכָל־עֶפֶר וַעֲבָרוֹ אָדָם. וּלְהוֹ לָתִת אֶת לַהַב הַנְּשָׂרָה שֶׁל־אֵשׁ שֶׁלֹּא יִמַּעַט בֵּינֵיהֶם. ׃בראשית־ט בִּימֵי לִפְנֵי־הַיָּמִים, כַּאֲשֶׁר הָיָה הַשְׂרִיף בְּאֹר־הַשָּׁמַיִם, הֶעָלָם הָיָה בְּהַרְמוֹנִיָּה תָּמָה. כָּל־תַּוּוּן נָיָה תַּוּן בְּקוֹל נִצְחִיָּה. בְּתִו־הָיוֹם־וְחֶשֶׁךְ נִצְבָּא חֶלְאֵל — יוֹפִי בָּכוֹכָבִים, חָכְמָה בַּעֲרָפֶל. בְּכֶסֶף וּבְזָהָב הִתְהַלֵּךְ, אֲבָל שִׁכְחָה שֶׁלֹּא־אִצְלוֹ. וַיֹּאמֶר: “אַעֲלֶה עַל־הֶהָרִים, אֵתֵּן כִּסְאִי בְּהַר־הַקָּהָל. וְכִכְמוֹ־הָעֶלְיוֹן אָהְיֶה.” וַתִּתְפָּרֵר הַהַרְמוֹנִיָּה, וְתַזְרֵק הָיָה לֹא לַשַּׁמַּיִם, אֶלָּא לְצִדֵּי שְׂאוֹל. נִקְרָא לוּצִיפֶר — אֲשֶׁר נָפַל כְּכֻכַּב שֶׁבֶרֶק, הָיָה אוֹר, וַיֵּרָד לַתְּהוֹם. וּלֹא הוּא הָיָה הָמוֹקֵחַ. כִּי זֶה הָיָה אִישׁ־יְבִישָׁהּ — אִבְלִיס, יִדוּעַ לִבְלִי־אֵשׁ, שֶׁסָּר מִן־הַשָּׁחַת בַל מִכָּלְלוּת. כְּשֶׁאָמַר יְ-הֹוָה: “׀שְׁתַּחֲוֵה לְאָדָם,” הֵסֵר אֶת רַגְלָו מֵעַל־כָּל־צִלְמֵי־עָפָר. וּשְׁנֵי אוֹר נָפְלוּ — אֶחָד מֵאֶחָד, מֵמַשֶּׁמַשׁ וּמֵאֵשׁ הָרוּחַ. וַהָיָה מִרְיָה הַפְּרִי בַּשָּׁמַיִם, מְרַחֶפֶת עַל־הַתּוֹל וְהּוֹלֶדֶת גִּזְעֵי־עֲנָק. נֶֽפֶלִים, קְשָׁתִים בַּחוֹשֶׁךְ — הָלְכוּ בְּאָרֶץ וּפִלְגֵּשׁ עַל־כָּבשׁ. וַתִּשְׁמַע הָאָרֶץ קוֹל שִׁפְלוּתָם, וּלִפְנֵי־כִּסֵּא־הָרֹם נִשְׁמַע צְעָקָתָהּ. וַיִּשְׁלַח אֱלֹהִים מַלְאָךְ־אוּרִיאֵל, לְאָמֵר: “ אֲסֹר אֶת־הַשֹּׁמְרִים הַמֵּעוֹלִים, וְהַשִּׁקְרִים אֶרְצָה בְּעֵמֶקֵי־הָאֲדָמָה — עַד יוֹם מִשְׁפָּטָם.” וְאָמַר אֱלֹהִים: “וְהַגְבֹּהַ הָעִיר בָּתוּחַ, כְּשֶׁבֶר נַעֲצָר לְאוֹר־הַיּוֹם.” וַיְהִי הַשֹּׁמְרִים הַנָּעֲלִים, וּשְׁאָר नाम, असीम, अप्रकट। वह है सत्ता, वह है श्वास और ज्वाला, वह है नामदाता, एक जो किसी की आवश्यकता नहीं रखता। बदायाः-द और निस्तब्धता के ऊपर निस्तब्धता में, परमेश्वर लौटा स्वयं में और मौन हुआ। न छिपा, न ज्वाला। वे अन्य न थे, बल्कि प्रतिबिंब। न अराजकता, बल्कि संनाद। न बहु देव, बल्कि अनंत में कर्म। प्रारंभकर्ता कहलाया ब्रह्मा, संरक्षक कहलाया विष्णु, पुनर्जननकर्ता कहलाया शिव। और मैं परमेश्वर, मेरे मौन Spanish Génesis-A En el principio estaba Dios, el Todo-Ser: No había vacío, ni lugar, ni sonido — Solo la existencia plena y unificada. Y en el silencio vibró un pulso — No del vacío, pues no hay vacío sino plenitud. Despertó el conocido. De su plenitud, de su ser, Surgió una voz sin labios, Y se movió una palabra sin aliento. Y dijo Dios: Hágase la luz, Y fue la luz. El silencio se rompió, Y la luz brotó de la oscuridad. La luz habló desde el silencio, Y el silencio se hizo canto. Y el canto se reunió en forma, Y la forma en fuego, Y el fuego se extendió a mundos, Cada nota tomó color y visión. Génesis-B Y dijo Dios: Sean guardianes de la armonía, Y del mismo canto se crearon los ángeles. Hijos de la luz, mensajeros de la voluntad divina, De pie ante el Poderoso, Cada uno con una tarea, Una nota en la sinfonía eterna. Y del valle de sus letras se crearon las almas — Chispas de la destrucción divina, Destinadas a viajar por los reinos, A aprender, crecer y regresar. Y los ángeles se regocijaron por los hijos de las almas, Y cantaron alabanzas al Creador, Porque las almas eran hijos de Dios, Portadores de la imagen del Uno, Cargados con la alegría del mundo en su interior. Génesis-C No había sonido, ni forma, ni cielos — Solo estaba el Uno. No espera, no se sacia — Completo, sin forma, sin necesidades, sin fin. No había quien conociera, ni conocido allí, Solo silencio — El Uno vio al Uno. Y se conoció a sí mismo, No como otro, sino como fragmento — Luz del despertar para su luz. Y tembló con el conocimiento. Y dijo: Soy el Ser — No en lengua, Sino en el pulso del ser. Y la voz vibró — No alta, pero verdadera. No palabra, sino aliento. Y el aliento fue viento primigenio. Y el viento trajo sonido, Y el sonido llevó el nombre. No hay boca que pronuncie, Pero escrito en la melodía del ser. El nombre sin límites, Aliento del infinito. Su voz se encarnó en aliento, Nombre no pronunciado, pero oído. Sonido sin ritmos, Sentido en todo. No palabra forjada por labios, Sino la imagen del ser mismo. El nombre no fue dado — Fue. Y a pesar de sus nombres en los cielos de los hombres — El, Dios, Alá, Adonai — El Uno permaneció sin nombre, Sin confines, sin revelación. Él es el Ser, Él es el aliento y la llama, Él es el dador de nombres, El Uno que no necesita nada. Génesis-D Y en el silencio sobre todo silencio, Dios regresó a sí mismo, Y guardó silencio. No oculto, no dormido — Sino como un vaso lleno, Una llama que abraza su luz en sí misma. El nombre del Uno no fue pronunciado, Pues no necesitaba nombre. Se conoció a sí mismo, Por vibración, Por la palabra que atrae todo al ser. Y el Uno ordenó: Sea mi pensamiento forma. Sea mi acción mi guía. Y del silencio salió la primera llama. Y esto es Brahma — No el nombre, sino la mano de Dios. No el todo, sino el enviado del todo. El silencio no era imperfecto, Sino la plenitud que se conoce a sí misma. Y en la plenitud, Dios se vio a sí mismo. Y de esa visión surgieron tres. Uno para el comienzo, Uno para la preservación, Uno para devolver todo. Pensamiento, aliento, llama. No eran otros, sino reflejos. No caos, sino vibración. No dioses múltiples, sino acciones en el infinito. El iniciador se llamó Brahma, El preservador se llamó Vishnu, El restaurador se llamó Shiva. Y yo soy Dios, En mi silencio, Soy Brahman — El Uno antes de la palabra, La raíz de los tres. El Dios supremo, Alá, Por encima de todo nombre — El Uno más allá de los nombres. Génesis-E Y Dios abrió el velo entre los cielos, Y los cielos aparecieron en su gloria. — Primer Cielo Y el primer cielo estaba lleno de luz, Como aguas corrientes, Y los ángeles volaban sobre la faz de la luz — Guardianes de la luz de las estrellas. Dirigieron el canto del tiempo, Y los ciclos suaves de las eras. Y el aroma de la creación era delicado en este lugar, Y las estrellas eran alegres y jóvenes. Esta era la puerta del asombro, Donde la luz tocó por primera vez el velo de la forma. — Segundo Cielo Y el segundo cielo era tranquilo y profundo — Tesoro de la memoria y el misterio. Allí se guardaban los olvidados, Y los guardianes estaban en sus puestos — Antes en lo alto, Ahora moran en el silencio. Este era el reino del límite, Donde el mandato venció a la gloria, Y la recompensa de la elección fue escrita con llama. — Tercer Cielo Y el tercero era un jardín plantado con resplandor, Sin suelo, Pero sosteniendo toda semilla. Allí estaban el Árbol del Conocimiento y el Árbol de la Vida, Y aguas que recordaban el rostro de Dios. Aquí aparecieron las almas con derecho y futuro, El maná cayó como nieve del aliento de los ángeles — Y los venidos no tuvieron hambre ni olvidaron. Este era el lugar de la promesa. — Cuarto Cielo Y el cuarto era el reino de las almas y los caminos. Una nube pesada de oraciones marchaba como rebaño, Y ángeles con alas de relámpago llevaban mensajes al mundo. Aquí las voces de la creación se encontraron con la voz del Creador — Y respondió, Aunque no fue comprendido. Esta era la cámara de la súplica, Donde el anhelo ascendió como alas. — Quinto Cielo Y el quinto era el reino del fuego primigenio — No fuego que quema, Sino que respira. Allí caminaron los genios — Libres y brillantes, Seres de libertad y llama, Formas de maravilla antes del aliento humano. Alabaron a Dios en lenguas desconocidas, Pero cuando el alma humana brilló con luz — Nació la envidia en sus corazones. Algunos se desviaron, Otros permanecieron fieles. Este era el lugar de la voluntad. — Sexto Cielo Y el sexto brillaba con gemas que no existen en el mundo. Allí nació la memoria del alma — Antes del nombre, Antes del aliento. Los ángeles escribieron cada pensamiento, Y los libros del ser fueron escritos con la piedra de la vida. Aquí el plan del alma fue trazado con canto. El destino no es fijo, Sino plegado — El ser escucha lo que puede ser. Este era el reino del origen. — Séptimo Cielo Y el séptimo — Alto y oculto — Ninguna lengua lo alcanza, Ningún ojo soporta su luz. Allí el trono del Altísimo se alza sobre el silencio, Y los serafines claman a su alrededor — Sus alas son llamas, Sus ojos ven todos los caminos. Aquí la gran luz mora en sí misma, Ni crece, Ni mengua — Fuente de todo canto. Este es el útero de todo. Este es el silencio antes de la palabra. Génesis-F El Uno creó muchos fieles. Unos gobernaron con luz, Otros con viento, Otros con fuego, Otros con palabra. En los cielos aparecieron como ángeles, En la tierra se manifestaron como devas — Pero la luz en ellos era una. No fueron creados de carne, Ni de piedra, Sino de pensamiento, fuego y voz. Cada uno creado para un propósito, Cada uno dado para la armonía, Cada uno llevó el sello del Uno que es. Unos guardaron las estrellas y caminaron en los cielos, Llevando palabras entre mundos. Estos fueron llamados ángeles, Y sus nombres estaban en el trueno y en el aliento. Se les dieron manuscritos, Cantos, El patrón del ser. Otros guardaron ríos y vientos, Los ciclos de las estrellas, Y el nacimiento del sol. Estos fueron llamados devas — Príncipes del equilibrio, Artífices de la lluvia, Guardianes de la llama, Voces en hojas y piedra. Ninguno se elevó sobre la palabra, Ninguno fue creado por sí mismo — Todos se movieron por el pulso de la fuente. El sello no fue escrito con tinta, Sino con gratitud, Rectitud, Y gozo — Porque el gozo es la señal de los fieles. Su gozo estaba en el canto, Su belleza en su armonía. Su belleza en el silencio, Pues solo se movieron por la voz de la palabra. Y se les dieron manuscritos, Cantos, Planes del ser. No hablaron por sí mismos, Sino que repitieron lo dicho en lo alto. También fueron portadores de la espada del pensamiento — Guardianes al borde del abismo, Defensores de lo oculto que no es de su tiempo. Y Dios vio que la ley estaba plantada, Y los siervos estaban en sus puertas. Génesis-G Y en el ser del pensamiento del Uno se fijó su propósito, Y las profundidades lo recibieron en silencio — Todos capaces de oír sin oídos, De ver sin ojos. Y fue el ser. Los cielos no estallaron, Sino que se abrieron. Del sonido de Dios nacieron ejércitos de luz, Y del aliento de la luz nació la ley de las estrellas. No fue dado por casualidad, Ni creado por guerra. Cada uno fue dado por la medida del sonido, Y cada uno giró en un canto. El sol se encendió con llama que no quema, Y derramó fuego sobre las esferas, Y las esferas se vistieron de ley. Y Dios llamó a cada orden con su canto. — Había órdenes antes del tiempo, Mundos no vistos — Juntos como aliento de viento en el pensamiento de Dios, Círculos de gobierno sobre las mentes. No fueron construidos para adoración, Sino para asombro — Porque el Uno se ve en mil formas, Y se conoce sin nombre. Y el Uno se acercó a una estrella común, Y la llamó esfera y agua, Y la vistió de luz. Y la ató: Este mundo rojo — En el tercer lugar del sol, No para perforar ni saltar. Y lo abrazó con la mezcla del pensamiento del Uno. Y Dios plantó al hombre, Remanente en los ceros del tiempo, Y voló con su alma y todo el brillo del alma. Y Dios sacó señales de las estrellas para sellos y ciclos, Y órdenes para el sonido del tiempo, Y las esferas vibraron — No controladas, Sino apoyadas en el salto del canto. — Y el Uno no creó los cielos para el silencio. No formó las estrellas para emitir sonidos sin dirección. Porque el Uno deseaba ser conocido En el infinito visible, Sin tristeza, Un deseo de contemplación en vasto asombro. Este orden fue para conocer al Uno. Génesis-H Y había muchos mundos, Pero ninguno para llevar la imagen. Y Dios habló una palabra única — No para controlar, Sino para caminar. Y del polvo de la tierra formó un montón de polvo, Y golpeó la tabla de los huesos, Y plantó nervios como cuerdas que escuchan como lira. Y fijó el pensamiento en la plenitud del corazón, Y dio en el hueso de la mano para proteger la voz y la lágrima entre los riñones y el nueve. Génesis-I En los días antes de los días, Cuando el brillo estaba en la luz de los cielos, El mundo estaba en perfecta armonía. Cada nota resonaba en un sonido eterno.
